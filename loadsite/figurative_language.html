Figurative Language — Quick Lesson

Short definitions, examples, and a tiny quiz to check understanding.

Simile
Compares two things using like or as.
Example: She was as brave as a lion.
Tip: Look for the words like or as.
Metaphor
Says one thing is another to show similarity.
Example: Time is a thief.
Tip: No "like" or "as" — it states a direct comparison.
Personification
Gives human qualities to non-human things.
Example: The wind whispered through the trees.
Hyperbole
An extreme exaggeration used for effect.
Example: I'm so hungry I could eat a horse.
Onomatopoeia
A word that imitates a sound.
Example: The bacon sizzled in the pan.
Alliteration
Repeating the same beginning sound in nearby words.
Example: Sammy sells seashells by the seashore.
Idiom
A phrase whose meaning isn't obvious from the words.
Example: It's raining cats and dogs. (means: it's raining heavily)
