Fractions — a friendly guide

Understand parts of a whole, equivalent fractions, simplifying, and quick tools

What is a fraction?

A fraction represents a part of a whole and has two parts: the numerator (top) and the denominator (bottom). Example: 3/4 means three out of four equal parts.

Types of fractions

Proper: numerator < denominator (e.g., 2/3).
Improper: numerator ≥ denominator (e.g., 7/4).
Mixed number: whole number + proper fraction (e.g., 1 3/4).

How to simplify (short)

Find the greatest common divisor (GCD) of numerator and denominator, then divide both by it. Example: 8/12 → GCD=4 → 2/3.

Tips

  • To add/subtract, get a common denominator.
  • To multiply, multiply numerators and denominators directly.
  • To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal (swap numerator/denominator).

Reference

Useful representations:

Fraction:   a / b
Mixed:      w a / b  (meaning w + a/b)
Decimal:    a ÷ b
